HouseHold

Renting website & app

Challenge

In renting processes, tenants feel low security, no confidence in realtors or homeowners, they are disenchanted, and some of them fear the unknown. The renting process is complicated, offers don’t represent reality and it is a nuisance for tenants having to show the house to new possible tenants.

Solution

Design a website & app to be user friendly by providing clear navigation, offering an easier process, giving more aids to tenants and owners and helping tenants to gain confidence and security in the renting process.

My role

UX designer designing HouseHold website & app from conception to delivery.

Date

May 2023 to July 2023

Process summary

Empathize

Deep understanding of the user. Who are the users? What are they expecting from the product? What are their pain points? What are their goals?

Define

Analyzing the data collected and the market. Concrete the issue to be solved.

Ideate

Exploring solutions for the issue to be solved and evaluate them.

Design

Paper wireframes turned into digital wireframes, turned into a low fidelity prototype to test on real users and get feedback to polish the design.

Test

The low fidelity prototype is tested by real users of the product. The prototype is polished, after that a high fidelity prototype is made and tested again if needed.

Bio

Julia is an office worker that has experience in renting, but she has too many bad experiences. She feels disenchanted, misled and vulnerable with the renting process, rented houses, realtors and home owners. She needs to find something that fits her needs, that give her more security and helps her gain confidence and more access to benefits.

Personas pain points

Low confidence

Offers don’t represent reality. Sometimes realtors and homeowners deceive tenants. This cause tenants to feel insecure, vulnerable and disenchanted.

Added responsibilities

Homeowners sometimes burden their tenants with responsibilities that are not their own, like showing the house to new tenants.

Complicated process

Usually, the website process of renting a house is complicated and cause the users to feel misled and confused. Tenants have to run and compete to have their chosen house with little margin.

Increased anxiety

Some users feel anxiety about changing homes and not finding a house equal or better to their current home. Not having guides in those websites to help users find something that meets their needs increase their unease.

Key findings & insights

Usability study parameters

  • Study type

    Unmoderated

  • Location

    Spain, remote

  • Participants

    5 participants

  • Length

    20 - 40 minutes

Key findings

  • Navigation hints

    Some actions success were not indicated when they were completed. This caused confusion to participants doubting if the action was completed.

  • Anxiety changing houses

    Some users have a lot of anxiety about changing houses and fear finding something worse. Similar searches section is added to help users find something that satisfy them.

  • Distrust

    Most participants don’t trust homeowners, realtors and renting websites. The rating section has been added to provide other users opinions and experiences and give security between users.
